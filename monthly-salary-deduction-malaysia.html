Monthly Salary Deduction in Malaysia

A simple guide to monthly salary deductions in Malaysia, including EPF, SOCSO, EIS and PCB for employees.

EPFSOCSOEISPCB

Direct answer

Monthly salary deduction in Malaysia includes statutory deductions (EPF, SOCSO, EIS, PCB) plus payroll-specific items such as unpaid leave, salary advance recovery, and allowance adjustments.

Full deduction landscape

Employees often focus only on EPF, but monthly payroll can include multiple deduction categories. Understanding each category helps explain why bank-in differs from one month to another.

Common monthly deductions table

How payroll teams usually process deductions

  1. Calculate earnings in current cycle.
  2. Apply statutory deductions based on payroll rules and wage classes.
  3. Apply policy deductions (advances, unpaid leave, etc.).
  4. Apply cut-off-based adjustments from prior period claims/errors.
  5. Finalize net pay and release payslip.

Scenario: why one month dropped RM400

A drop may come from combined factors: 1 unpaid leave day, higher PCB after overtime, and a salary advance recovery in the same payroll month.

Good employee checklist

  • Save monthly payslips for comparisons.
  • Track non-statutory deduction lines separately.
  • Ask HR if a line item is recurring or one-off.
  • Check whether allowance lines are taxable or reimbursable.

Extended FAQ and practical notes

For employees in Malaysia, payroll outcomes depend on both statutory rules and company payroll implementation details such as cut-off dates, attendance lock dates, and payment cycles. Keep your monthly payslips and compare line-by-line over time.

  • Check whether your payroll is calendar-month or cut-off-cycle based.
  • Separate recurring deductions from one-off deductions.
  • Document HR clarifications for future month comparisons.
  • Use rolling 3-month net averages for safer personal budgeting.

Frequently asked practical questions

Q: Can deductions change without salary change? Yes, especially PCB and one-off adjustments.

Q: Should I budget based on gross? No, always budget on conservative net estimates.

Q: Why is first month lower? Proration and cut-off timing are common reasons.

Q: What if deduction seems wrong? Request payroll breakdown and supporting schedules from HR.
