Why Is My Salary Less Than My Offer Letter in Malaysia?

Learn why your bank-in salary may be lower than your offer letter amount in Malaysia after EPF, SOCSO, EIS and PCB deductions.

EPFSOCSOEISPCB

Direct answer

Your salary can be lower than your offer letter amount because the offer is usually gross pay, while bank-in salary is net pay after EPF, SOCSO, EIS, PCB and other payroll adjustments.

Why this happens so often

Offer letters in Malaysia typically present gross monthly salary. Actual payout on payday reflects payroll reality: statutory deductions plus any month-specific adjustments.

First-month salary example

If you join on the 16th, your first month may be prorated. Even before statutory deductions, gross payable is already less than full-month amount.

Last-month salary example

In final service month, payroll may include leave encashment, final claims, or offsets. Net pay can be higher or lower depending on settlement items.

Scenario guide

Scenario A: “I got RM500 less than expected”

Usually a combination of EPF+SOCSO+EIS+PCB and possibly unpaid leave or cut-off delay.

Scenario B: “My colleague earns same gross but gets different net”

PCB profile and personal declarations can differ, leading to different monthly tax deductions.

Scenario C: “Offer included allowance, but not in this month’s payout”

Some allowances are conditional, variable, or tied to attendance and policy rules.

How to discuss with HR/payroll

  1. Request detailed payslip breakdown by line item.
  2. Ask whether offer figure is gross or net (usually gross).
  3. Ask if any unpaid leave, advance recovery, or cut-off items applied.
  4. Confirm variable allowance criteria and payout timing.

Extended FAQ and practical notes

For employees in Malaysia, payroll outcomes depend on both statutory rules and company payroll implementation details such as cut-off dates, attendance lock dates, and payment cycles. Keep your monthly payslips and compare line-by-line over time.

  • Check whether your payroll is calendar-month or cut-off-cycle based.
  • Separate recurring deductions from one-off deductions.
  • Document HR clarifications for future month comparisons.
  • Use rolling 3-month net averages for safer personal budgeting.

Frequently asked practical questions

Q: Can deductions change without salary change? Yes, especially PCB and one-off adjustments.

Q: Should I budget based on gross? No, always budget on conservative net estimates.

Q: Why is first month lower? Proration and cut-off timing are common reasons.

Q: What if deduction seems wrong? Request payroll breakdown and supporting schedules from HR.
