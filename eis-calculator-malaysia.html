What is EIS?

EIS (Employment Insurance System) is a social security scheme administered by PERKESO (Pertubuhan Keselamatan Sosial) in Malaysia. Introduced under the Employment Insurance System Act 2017, EIS provides financial support to eligible employees who lose their jobs involuntarily, such as through retrenchment, company closure or restructuring.

When an insured employee faces job loss, EIS may provide temporary financial assistance, job search allowances, and access to retraining programmes. Both the employee and employer contribute a small percentage of the employee's wages towards the scheme each month.

EIS Contribution Rates

Under current EIS rules, the monthly contribution rate is typically 0.2% of the employee's wages, shared equally between the employer and the employee. This means each party contributes 0.2% separately, resulting in a total combined rate of 0.4% per month.

Employee0.2%RM 6.00
Employer0.2%RM 6.00
Total0.4%RM 12.00

The maximum salary subject to EIS deduction is RM 6,000 per month. Any amount above this ceiling is not subject to EIS contribution, so the maximum employee EIS deduction is RM 12.00 per month.

Common EIS Calculation Mistakes

  • Ignoring the wage ceiling: Some people mistakenly apply the EIS rate to their full salary even when it exceeds RM 6,000. Contributions are only calculated up to the RM 6,000 ceiling, so salaries above this limit still cap at RM 12.00 (employee share).
  • Confusing EIS with SOCSO rates: EIS and SOCSO have different contribution rates and wage ceilings. Applying SOCSO rates to EIS calculations — or vice versa — leads to inaccurate estimates. Always check the specific PERKESO schedule for each scheme.
  • Not accounting for part-month wages: If you start or leave a job partway through the month, your EIS contribution should be based on your actual wages earned in that month, not a full month's salary. Prorating incorrectly can cause discrepancies on your payslip.
  • Forgetting employer portion: EIS is a shared contribution. Employees sometimes only consider their own deduction and overlook the employer's matching contribution, which is important for understanding total employment cost.

EIS calculator FAQ

What is EIS (Employment Insurance System) in Malaysia?

EIS is a social security scheme managed by PERKESO that provides temporary financial support to employees who lose their jobs involuntarily. It covers situations such as retrenchment, company closure, and certain types of resignation. EIS also offers retraining programmes to help affected workers find new employment.

Who needs to contribute to EIS?

All Malaysian citizens and permanent residents who are employed in the private sector and earn a monthly wage of RM 30 or more are required to contribute to EIS. Both the employee and the employer must make monthly contributions, regardless of whether the employment is permanent, temporary, or contract-based.

How much is the EIS contribution rate?

The current EIS contribution rate is 0.2% of wages for the employee and 0.2% for the employer, totalling 0.4% per month. The rate is applied to the employee's monthly wages up to the maximum wage ceiling of RM 6,000. For a RM 3,000 monthly salary, the employee pays RM 6.00 and the employer pays RM 6.00.

What is the maximum salary subject to EIS deduction?

The EIS wage ceiling is RM 6,000 per month. If your monthly salary exceeds RM 6,000, only the first RM 6,000 is used for EIS calculation. This means the maximum monthly EIS deduction for an employee is RM 12.00 (0.2% of RM 6,000), regardless of how much you earn above the ceiling.

How do I claim EIS benefits if I lose my job?

To claim EIS benefits, you must register with PERKESO and submit a claim through the myFutureJobs portal or at a PERKESO branch, typically within 60 days of your last working day. You will need documents such as your termination letter, IC, and bank statements. Approved claimants may receive job-search allowance for up to six months, subject to eligibility conditions.

What is the difference between EIS and SOCSO?

While both EIS and SOCSO are administered by PERKESO, they serve different purposes. SOCSO (also known as PERKESO) primarily covers employment injuries, occupational diseases, and invalidity. EIS specifically provides job-loss protection and retraining support. They have separate contribution rates and calculations, so they appear as distinct items on your payslip.

Is EIS mandatory for all employees in Malaysia?

EIS is mandatory for most private sector employees who are Malaysian citizens or permanent residents, aged between 16 and 60, and earning RM 30 or more per month. However, certain categories of workers such as domestic servants, government employees, and self-employed individuals are generally not covered under EIS unless they opt into a voluntary scheme.

Does a self-employed person need to pay EIS?

Standard EIS contributions apply only to employees in the private sector. Self-employed individuals, freelancers, and gig workers are not automatically covered under the mandatory EIS scheme. However, PERKESO has introduced voluntary contribution schemes such as SIP (Self-Employment Social Protection) which allow self-employed persons to opt in for limited social security coverage, including some EIS-like benefits.

Is EIS the same as SOCSO?

No. EIS and SOCSO are different payroll contribution items. Both may appear on a Malaysian employee payslip, but they are calculated separately.

Is this the official EIS calculation?
