What the Deductions Mean

EPF / KWSP

EPF, also known as KWSP, is Malaysia's retirement savings scheme. The employee contribution is usually deducted from wages, while the employer contributes an additional amount on top of the employee's salary. EPF helps build long-term retirement savings. Actual EPF treatment may depend on the type of wages and the latest official KWSP rules.

SOCSO / PERKESO

SOCSO, also known as PERKESO, provides social security protection for eligible employees. It may cover employment injury, occupational disease, invalidity and related benefits, depending on eligibility and official rules. SOCSO contributions are usually based on wage tiers rather than a simple flat percentage.

EIS

EIS stands for Employment Insurance System. It is administered by PERKESO and provides support for eligible employees who lose their job involuntarily, such as through retrenchment or company closure. EIS is usually a small monthly deduction, but it can provide useful job-loss protection when conditions are met.

PCB / Monthly Tax Deduction

PCB, or Potongan Cukai Berjadual, is Malaysia's monthly tax deduction system. Your employer may deduct PCB from your salary and remit it to LHDN as part of your annual income tax obligation. PCB can vary depending on your income level, tax category, reliefs, bonus, commission and other taxable income.

Why Your Actual Take-Home Pay May Differ

This calculator provides an estimate only. Your actual take-home pay may differ because:

  • Your salary may include allowances that are treated differently for payroll purposes.
  • You may have selected a different EPF contribution rate.
  • Your SOCSO amount may depend on the official wage tier.
  • Your PCB may depend on personal tax reliefs declared to your employer.
  • You may have other deductions such as PTPTN, advances, unpaid leave or company deductions.
  • Official EPF, SOCSO, EIS or PCB rules may change over time.

Always compare the estimate with your actual payslip. For the latest official rules, confirm with KWSP, PERKESO, LHDN or a qualified payroll or tax professional.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much is deducted from salary in Malaysia?

Common salary deductions in Malaysia may include EPF, SOCSO, EIS and PCB. The total deduction depends on your salary level, payroll setup, tax category and reliefs. Use this calculator as an estimate only.

Is EPF calculated from gross salary?

EPF is generally calculated from wages as defined by EPF rules. This may include basic salary and certain fixed allowances, but some payments may be excluded. Confirm the applicable wages base with your employer or KWSP.

Is SOCSO the same as EIS?

No. SOCSO and EIS are separate schemes administered by PERKESO. SOCSO generally relates to employment injury and invalidity protection, while EIS relates to job-loss support for eligible employees.

Why is my PCB higher this month?

PCB may be higher in a month when you receive a bonus, commission, back pay or when your employer recalculates your annual tax projection. Your declared tax reliefs can also affect PCB.

Why is my take-home pay different from the calculator estimate?

Online calculators use assumptions. Your actual take-home pay may differ due to allowance treatment, selected EPF rate, tax reliefs, other deductions, unpaid leave or payroll processing differences.

What should I check on my payslip?

Check your gross salary, EPF deduction, SOCSO deduction, EIS deduction, PCB amount, allowances, unpaid leave, overtime, bonus, and any other deductions. If something looks wrong, ask your HR or payroll team.
