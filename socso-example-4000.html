SOCSO Contribution Example for RM4,000 Salary in Malaysia

What Is SOCSO?

SOCSO (Social Security Organisation), also known as PERKESO (Pertubuhan Keselamatan Sosial), is a Malaysian government agency responsible for providing social security protection to employees. Under the Employees' Social Security Act 1969, most employers in Malaysia are required to register their employees and make monthly SOCSO contributions.

SOCSO provides financial protection in the event of employment-related injuries, occupational diseases, invalidity, and death. For most private sector employees, SOCSO coverage falls under First Category, which includes both Employment Injury Scheme and Invalidity Pension Scheme.

How SOCSO Contributions Work

Unlike EPF, which uses a flat percentage, SOCSO contributions are based on official wage bands published by PERKESO. Each wage band covers a salary range and specifies a fixed contribution amount for both the employee and the employer. This system means:

  • Employees within the same wage band pay the same contribution, regardless of small salary differences.
  • When your salary crosses into a higher band, your contribution increases by a fixed amount.
  • There is a maximum wage ceiling (currently RM5,000 per month for First Category).

SOCSO Rule: Both employee and employer contribute to SOCSO. The employer's share is significantly higher than the employee's share.

What SOCSO Covers

Under the First Category, SOCSO provides two main types of protection:

  • Employment Injury Scheme: Covers work-related accidents, occupational diseases, and commuting accidents. Benefits include free medical treatment at SOCSO-panel clinics, temporary disablement benefit (a percentage of salary during recovery), permanent disablement benefit, and dependants' benefit in case of death.
  • Invalidity Pension Scheme: Provides a monthly pension if the employee becomes permanently invalid (unable to work in any occupation). The pension amount depends on the employee's contribution period and average wages.

SOCSO does not cover unemployment, which is handled separately by EIS (Employment Insurance System). It also does not cover non-work-related illnesses or retirement savings (which is EPF's role).

SOCSO Wage Ceiling and Higher Earners

The current SOCSO wage ceiling for First Category is RM5,000 per month. This means that if you earn RM5,000 or more, your employee SOCSO contribution is capped at the maximum band amount. An employee earning RM8,000 pays the same SOCSO as one earning RM5,000. This ceiling is important to understand because:

  • Higher earners do not receive proportionally more SOCSO protection.
  • Your SOCSO deduction may stay the same even after a significant salary increment.
  • The ceiling is periodically reviewed by the government and may be adjusted in future.

Common Mistakes About SOCSO

  • Confusing SOCSO with EIS: SOCSO covers employment injury and invalidity, while EIS covers job loss. Both are separate deductions. Check your payslip to ensure both are being deducted correctly.
  • Not checking SOCSO registration: Every employee should be registered with SOCSO within 30 days of starting work. If you are not registered, you may not be covered in case of a workplace accident.
  • Assuming SOCSO is a percentage: Many people mistakenly calculate SOCSO as a flat percentage of salary. In reality, it follows wage bands with fixed amounts.
  • Ignoring the wage ceiling: Employees earning above RM5,000 sometimes expect higher SOCSO contributions with each increment, not realising the ceiling limits their contributions.
  • Not knowing about self-contribution: Freelancers and self-employed individuals in certain sectors can voluntarily contribute to SOCSO under the Self-Employment Social Security Scheme (SKSPS).

Frequently Asked Questions

How much SOCSO do I pay if I earn RM4,000 a month in Malaysia?

For a RM4,000 monthly salary under SOCSO First Category, the employee contribution is approximately RM48.50 per month based on the official SOCSO wage band for the RM4,000 salary range. The employer contribution is higher at approximately RM141.30. These amounts are determined by PERKESO wage bands, not a flat percentage.

What does SOCSO First Category cover?

SOCSO First Category covers employment injury (including workplace accidents and occupational diseases) and invalidity. If you suffer a work-related injury or become permanently disabled, SOCSO provides medical benefits, temporary or permanent disability payments, and rehabilitation support. First Category is the standard coverage for most employees.

What is the difference between SOCSO and EIS?

SOCSO (PERKESO) provides protection against employment injury and invalidity, while EIS (Employment Insurance System) provides short-term financial support if you lose your job through no fault of your own. Both are separate deductions on your payslip. SOCSO contributions are based on fixed wage bands, while EIS is calculated as 0.2% of your gross monthly salary.

Does SOCSO use a percentage or fixed wage bands?

SOCSO uses fixed wage bands, not a flat percentage like EPF. PERKESO publishes a schedule of salary ranges with corresponding contribution amounts for both employee and employer. This means the contribution stays the same within a band, even if your actual salary increases slightly. For example, RM4,000 and RM4,050 may fall in the same band with the same contribution.

Is there a wage ceiling for SOCSO contributions in Malaysia?

Yes. SOCSO has a maximum wage ceiling (currently RM5,000 per month for First Category contributions). Any salary above this ceiling is not subject to additional SOCSO contributions. This means high earners above RM5,000 pay the same SOCSO amount as someone earning exactly RM5,000. The ceiling is periodically reviewed by PERKESO and may be adjusted.

Can I check my SOCSO contribution records online?

Yes. You can check your SOCSO contribution records through the PERKESO official portal or the iPERKESO mobile app. Your employer is required to register you with SOCSO and make monthly contributions. If you notice any missing contributions or discrepancies on your payslip, you should raise the issue with your HR department or contact PERKESO directly.
