What SOCSO / PERKESO means

SOCSO (Social Security Organisation), commonly referred to as PERKESO in Malaysia, provides social security protection for eligible employees. Contributions help support benefits related to employment injury and invalidity protection under applicable schemes. In payroll operations, SOCSO is one of the mandatory statutory items that employers and employees monitor monthly.

Why SOCSO is not always a simple flat percentage

Unlike a pure percentage model, SOCSO is typically tied to contribution schedules and wage classes. That means deduction behavior may look “step-based” instead of perfectly linear. Two employees with similar salaries near a wage band threshold may observe values that do not move exactly by the same percentage from one month to another. This is a common source of confusion when employees try to calculate by hand.

Employee vs employer SOCSO contribution overview

SOCSO generally includes an employee share and an employer share. The employee share is the part visible as a deduction from pay, while the employer share is paid by the company. For take-home budgeting, employees should focus on employee SOCSO; for total compensation or payroll cost, include both parts.

How SOCSO differs from EPF and EIS

EPF is primarily retirement savings, while SOCSO is social security protection. EIS is employment insurance focused on job-loss related support under qualifying conditions. Although all three appear in payroll deductions, their objectives and calculation logic are different. SOCSO often follows table-based structure; EPF commonly uses contribution-rate logic; EIS has its own schedule and ceiling treatment.

Common SOCSO calculation mistakes

  • Assuming one flat SOCSO percentage across all wage levels.
  • Ignoring wage category thresholds and official contribution classes.
  • Mixing up SOCSO and EIS values on the payslip.
  • Using annual package numbers instead of monthly contributable wages.
  • Forgetting prorated effects for joining, resignation, or unpaid leave periods.

SOCSO calculator FAQ

1) Is this SOCSO value exact for payroll filing?

No. It is a planning estimate only.

2) Why can SOCSO look capped at higher salaries?

Official schedules may apply wage ceilings or class-based values, causing contributions to plateau in certain ranges.

3) Is SOCSO the same as EIS?

No. They are separate statutory components with different purposes and calculation tables.

4) Does employer SOCSO reduce my take-home pay?

No. Only employee SOCSO is deducted from your salary; employer SOCSO is an additional employer cost.

5) Can SOCSO change if my allowances change?

Yes, if contributable wages move into a different wage class or table band.

6) Why does SOCSO differ between months with unpaid leave?

Reduced payable wages can affect contribution category and amount.

7) Can I use this for offer comparison?

Yes, for quick estimation. Then confirm with official payroll breakdown before accepting an offer.

8) Where do I verify current contribution tables?

Check official PERKESO resources and your company payroll policy for the latest applicable schedules.
