Salary Deductions Malaysia: EPF, SOCSO, EIS and PCB

Understanding salary deductions is one of the most important aspects of managing your finances in Malaysia. Every month, a portion of your gross salary is deducted before it reaches your bank account. These deductions include mandatory statutory contributions required by Malaysian law, as well as optional deductions that depend on your employer and personal choices. This guide explains each major deduction category, how the rates work and what you can expect to see on your payslip.

Overview of Common Salary Deductions in Malaysia

For most employees working in the private sector in Malaysia, the following deductions appear on the monthly payslip:

  • EPF (Kumpulan Wang Simpanan Pekerja / KWSP) – Mandatory retirement savings contribution
  • SOCSO (Pertubuhan Keselamatan Sosial / PERKESO) – Social security for workplace injuries and invalidity
  • EIS (Skim Insurans Pekerjaan / SIP) – Employment insurance for job-loss protection
  • PCB (Potongan Cukai Bulanan) – Monthly tax deduction via LHDN's MTD Schedule
  • Other deductions – Zakat, salary advances, loan instalments, insurance premiums (where applicable)

EPF (Employees Provident Fund)

The EPF is Malaysia's flagship retirement savings scheme established under the EPF Act 1991. It requires both the employee and employer to contribute a percentage of the employee's monthly salary to a retirement account managed by EPF.

  • Employee contribution rate: 11% of monthly salary (default). Employees can optionally elect to reduce this to a lower percentage.
  • Employer contribution rate: 12% or 13% depending on the employee's salary level. For salaries above RM5,000, the employer rate is 12%.
  • Reduced rates for older employees: Employees aged 60–75 contribute at 5.5% (employee) / 6.5% (employer).
  • Wage ceiling: EPF contributions apply to the first RM100,000 of monthly wages (practically, this covers all standard salaries).
  • Who pays: The employee's share is deducted from salary. The employer's share is paid on top of the salary and does not reduce your take-home pay.

Remember: your employer's EPF contribution is additional to your gross salary — it is not deducted from your pay.

SOCSO (Social Security Organisation / PERKESO)

SOCSO provides social security protection to Malaysian employees under the Employees' Social Security Act 1969. The most relevant scheme for most employees is the First Category:

  • First Category: Covers Employment Injury (accidents and occupational diseases) and Invalidity Pension. This is the default for permanent employees.
  • Second Category: Covers Employment Injury only, applicable to employees aged 55 and above who are not eligible for Invalidity Pension.
  • Employee rate (First Category): 0.5% of monthly salary.
  • Employer rate (First Category): 1.25% of monthly salary.
  • Wage ceiling: Contributions apply to wages up to RM5,000 per month.

SOCSO benefits include free medical treatment at panel clinics for workplace injuries, temporary or permanent disablement benefits and survivors' pension for dependants if the employee passes away due to a work-related cause.

EIS (Employment Insurance System / SIP)

EIS was introduced in 2018 under the Employment Insurance System Act 2017 to provide a safety net for employees who lose their jobs. Key details include:

  • Purpose: Provides temporary financial assistance (job search allowance, reduced income allowance, training allowance) for up to 6 months.
  • Employee contribution: 0.2% of monthly salary.
  • Employer contribution: 0.2% of monthly salary.
  • Wage ceiling for employee: Capped at RM4,000 monthly wages (the employee's maximum monthly contribution is RM8.00).
  • Eligibility: The employee must have contributed for at least 9 months in the 15 months before the job loss.

PCB (Monthly Tax Deduction / MTD)

PCB (Potongan Cukai Bulanan) is the mechanism used by employers to deduct income tax from employees' salaries each month, as required under Section 107B of the Income Tax Act 1967.

  • How it works: Your employer uses the LHDN MTD Schedule (commonly known as the PCB Schedule or Kaedah Potongan Cukai Bulanan) to determine the monthly tax deduction based on your salary, marital status and number of children.
  • Relationship to annual tax filing: PCB is not your final tax. It is an advance payment. When you file your annual BE or B form with LHDN, your actual tax liability is calculated and the total PCB paid during the year is credited. You may receive a refund or owe additional tax.
  • Taxable income vs gross salary: PCB is calculated on taxable income (gross salary minus EPF employee contribution and approved deductions), not gross salary.

Other Possible Deductions

Besides the four main statutory deductions, you may see the following items on your payslip depending on your personal situation and employer policies:

  • Zakat: Mandatory for Muslim employees who qualify; deducted before PCB to reduce tax liability.
  • Salary advance recovery: If you received an advance payment, instalments may be deducted monthly.
  • Staff loan repayment: Some employers offer loans (computer, housing, education) that are repaid through salary deductions.
  • Insurance premiums: Group term life, medical or personal accident insurance with written employee authorisation.
  • Club or society fees: Contributions to staff associations or cooperative societies.
  • Loss or damage recovery: Only permitted with a written authorisation from the employee and subject to Employment Act limits.

Common Mistakes When Understanding Salary Deductions

  • Confusing employee and employer deductions: The employer's EPF, SOCSO and EIS contributions are paid on top of your salary — they do not come out of your pocket. Many employees mistakenly think their take-home loss equals the total contribution amount.
  • Assuming PCB is your final tax: PCB is only an advance. Your actual tax liability is determined when you file your annual return. You may owe more or receive a refund.
  • Forgetting that EPF reduces taxable income: Your PCB is calculated on salary minus EPF employee contribution, not on full gross salary. Electing a lower EPF rate increases your PCB.
  • Not checking payslips regularly: Deduction errors can accumulate over months. Always compare your payslip EPF and SOCSO figures against your official online statements.
  • Ignoring zakat benefits: Muslim employees who pay zakat through salary deduction can reduce PCB liability — this is a legitimate tax relief.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is EPF and how does the employee contribution work in Malaysia?

The Employees Provident Fund (EPF or KWSP) is Malaysia's mandatory retirement savings scheme. Employees contribute a percentage of their monthly salary (default 11%) while employers contribute 12% or 13% depending on salary level. These combined contributions are saved in your EPF account and earn annual dividends. Employees aged 60 and above contribute at reduced rates.

What does SOCSO cover and who is required to contribute?

SOCSO (PERKESO) provides social security protection including medical benefits for workplace injuries, invalidity pension and survivors' pension. All Malaysian employees earning above RM30 per month must be registered under the First Category (Employment Injury and Invalidity Scheme). Foreign workers are covered under specific schemes. Employers bear a larger share of the SOCSO contribution compared to employees.

What is EIS and how does it protect employees who lose their jobs?

The Employment Insurance System (EIS or SIP) provides temporary financial assistance to employees who lose their jobs through no fault of their own. Covered employees can receive job search allowances, reduced income allowance and training allowances for up to 6 months. Both employee and employer contribute 0.2% each of the employee's monthly wages, with the employee's share capped at RM4,000.

How is PCB different from my annual income tax filing?

PCB (Potongan Cukai Bulanan) is a monthly tax deduction deducted by your employer from each payroll based on LHDN's MTD Schedule. It is an advance payment towards your annual income tax liability. When you file your annual tax return (BE form), the total PCB deducted throughout the year is offset against your actual tax payable. If too much PCB was deducted, you receive a refund; if too little, you pay the balance.

Can my employer make deductions from my salary besides EPF, SOCSO, EIS and PCB?

Yes, but only under specific circumstances permitted by the Employment Act 1955. Permitted non-statutory deductions include salary advances, recovery of overpayments, contributions to approved provident or cooperative schemes, and deductions authorised in writing by the employee (such as insurance premiums or loan repayments). Unauthorised deductions are illegal and can be reported to the Labour Department.

How can I check whether the deductions on my Malaysian payslip are correct?

You can verify EPF contributions by logging into your EPF i-Akaun and checking the monthly statements. SOCSO contributions can be checked through the PERKESO portal. For PCB, compare the deducted amount against the LHDN MTD Schedule based on your salary category, number of children and spouse status. If you notice discrepancies, report them to your HR department immediately and file a complaint with the relevant authority if unresolved.
